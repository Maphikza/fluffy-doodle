INSURANCE ACT 18 OF 2017

This Act provides a framework for the regulation and supervision of insurance companies in South Africa. It also introduces a framework for microinsurance to promote financial inclusion. Finally, it replaces parts of the Long-term Insurance Act, 1998, and the Short-term Insurance Act, 1998.

The definitions section of this act explains key words and phrases used throughout the document.
"Accident" refers to an event that is external, violent, unexpected, and visible.
"Ancillary own funds" refers to items that a company may use to absorb losses, excluding items that have already been used or paid for.
"Associate" has the same meaning as it does in the International Financial Reporting Standards issued by the International Accounting Standards Board.
"Auditing Profession Act" refers to the Auditing Profession Act of 2005.
"Auditor" refers to someone who is registered under the Auditing Profession Act.
"Basic own funds" refers to the excess of assets over liabilities, including technical provisions that constitute the liability of the insurer arising from insurance obligations.
"Board of directors" refers to the governing body of a company or other organization.
"Branch of a foreign reinsurer" refers to an operating entity of a foreign reinsurer that is not a separate legal entity.
An insurer that only covers first-party risks is called a "captive insurer".
An insurer that only does business through cell structures is called a "cell captive insurer".
A "cell structure" is an arrangement where someone (the cell owner) has a stake in a specific class or type of shares of an insurer, which is separate from other types of shares. The cell owner is entitled to a share of the profits and losses from the stake, based on the insurance business done through the insurer. Insurance business placed or done with the insurer is contractually separate from the insurer's other business, as long as the insurer is not being wound down.
"Commercial lines" refers to non-life insurance business done for something other than personal lines. The Companies Act is a 2008 South African law that deals with companies.
"Control," in relation to the definition of controlling company and sections 10, 17, and 22(2) of the Companies Act, refers to a company's power over another company.
A "control function" is a function that a financial sector regulator performs.
A "controlling company" is a holding company of, or another company that controls, an insurance group located in South Africa that has been designated and licensed under Part 4 and Chapter 4 of the Companies Act, respectively.
The "Co-operatives Act" is a 2005 South African law that deals with co-operatives.
A "director" is a member of a board of directors and any person who can take the place of a member of the board.
A "death event" is when a person or an unborn person dies.
A "disability event" is when a person becomes so physically or mentally impaired that they can't continue their employment or own occupation, participate in any other occupation, or carry out normal activities of life.
"Discretionary participation features" are insurance obligations under a life insurance policy that make up a significant portion of the total insurance obligations, that the insurer has discretion over, and that are based on the performance of a specified pool of policies, investment returns, or the profit or loss of the insurer.
"Eligible own funds" are the basic own funds plus any ancillary own funds approved by the Prudential Authority (the regulatory body) as meeting the prescribed criteria for such funds. The total is adjusted according to the tiering restrictions that are in place.
"Encumber" means any pledge, restriction or limitation (including any contractual obligation that must be fulfilled before a contractual right may be exercised) that limits access to, or the use or disposal of, an asset.
"Financial inclusion" means providing financial services to those who do not have access to them.
The "Financial Institutions (Protection of Funds) Act" is a law that protects people's money in financial institutions.
The "Financial Sector Code" is a code of conduct for businesses in the financial sector, issued in terms of the Broad-Based Black Economic Empowerment Act.
The "Financial Sector Conduct Authority" is a regulatory body for the financial sector.
The "Financial Sector Regulation Act" is a law that regulates the financial sector.
"First party risks" are the risks of the main company or cell owner, and the risks of the group of companies that they are part of, or any associates or joint arrangements of those companies.
"Fit and proper requirements" are qualities that a key person or significant owner must have in order to be suitable for their role. These include honesty, integrity and competence (including experience, qualifications and knowledge).
A "foreign branch of an insurer" is an operating entity of an insurer that is part of the insurer in terms of its organisation and is not a legal entity separate from the insurer, but is established outside of the Republic.
A "foreign reinsurer" is an institution authorised and supervised by a regulatory authority to perform business similar to reinsurance business as defined in this Act under the laws of a country other than the Republic.
A "governing body" is a person or group of people who manage, control, or formulate policy and strategy for an insurer or controlling company. This includes people who are elected or not elected, and who direct the insurer's affairs or have the authority to exercise the company's powers and functions. A "group of companies" is any group of related companies, including a group of companies as defined in the Companies Act.
A "head of a control function" is a person appointed by a controlling company to oversee a particular area of the company's operations. This includes people who are appointed to oversee financial, compliance, or risk management functions.
A "health event" is an event relating to the mental or physical health of a person, or an unborn child. This does not include events relating to disability.
"Insurance business" refers to life insurance or non-life insurance conducted in the Republic, and includes reinsurance business.
An "insurance group" is a group of entities designated by the Prudential Authority under section 10.
"Insurance obligations" are all obligations under or arising from insurance policies, other than obligations of the policyholder. This includes obligations to pay one or more sums of money, render services, or meet any other obligations. In the case of life insurance policies, this also includes any guarantees and discretionary participation features.
An "insurance policy" is a life insurance policy or a non-life insurance policy.
An "insurer" is a person licensed to conduct insurance business under this Act. This includes Lloyd's, a Lloyd's underwriter, and a reinsurer.
"Inter-related" has the meaning defined in section 1 of the Companies Act.
An "intra-group transaction" is any arrangement or agreement in which an insurer relies on another person within the same insurance group, or a related or inter-related person, for the fulfilment of an obligation.
A "joint arrangement" is an arrangement set out in the International Financial Reporting Standards issued by the International Accounting Standards Board or a successor body.
A "juristic person" is a legal entity that has separate legal rights from its members, as defined in the Financial Sector Regulation Act.
"Key person" means a director, senior manager, head of a control function, auditor, trustee of a trust, or representative or deputy representative of Lloyd's or a branch of a foreign reinsurer.
"Life event" means the event of a person's life beginning, continuing, or having continued for a period.
"Life insurance business" means any activity conducted with the purpose of entering into or meeting insurance obligations under a life insurance policy.
A "life insurance policy" is an arrangement under which a person agrees to meet insurance obligations in return for receiving premiums, and can include things like renewals or variations of the arrangement.
The "life insured" is the person to whom an insurance policy relates, and who may or may not be the policyholder.
"Lloyd's" is an association of persons incorporated by the Lloyd's Act of 1871, passed by the Parliament of the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland.
A "Lloyd's underwriter" is a member or non-member of Lloyd's who undertakes to insure something.
"Microinsurance business" means insurance business conducted in respect of certain classes and sub-classes of insurance business set out in Schedule 2, where the aggregate value of the insurance obligations under all insurance policies issued by the same insurer to the same policyholder does not exceed certain maximum amounts.
A "microinsurer" is an insurer licensed to conduct only microinsurance business.
"Minister" means the Minister of Finance.

3. Objective of Act Back to Top

The objective of this Act is to, in a manner consistent with the Constitution of the Republic of South Africa, 1996, promote the maintenance of a fair, safe and stable insurance market for the benefit and protection of policyholders, by establishing a legal framework for the prudential regulation and supervision of insurers and insurance groups that ‚Äî

The goal of this act is to help insurance companies stay fair, safe, and stable, which will in turn protect insurance customers and make it easier for them to get insurance. The act also promotes transformation of the insurance industry and contributes to the stability of the financial system.
